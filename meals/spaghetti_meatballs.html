Spaghetti & Meatballs

A hearty meal the whole family will enjoy

This dish will take about 1 hour to cook

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb Ground Meat
  • 1/2 Cup Panko Breadcrumbs
  • 4 Cloves Garlic Minced
  • 2 Cups and 1 Tsp Salt
  • 1 Tsp Ground Black Pepper
  • 1 Tsp Ground Red Pepper
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/4 Cup Grated Parmesan
  • 1/4 Cup Parsley
  • 1 Can San Marzano Tomatoes
  • 2 Basil Leaves
  • 1/2 lb Spaghetti
  • 3 Tbsp Olive Oil

Instructions

Spaghetti

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil
  2. Add the 2 cups of salt to the water and let dissolve
  3. Add the 1/2 lb of spaghetti to the boiling water
  4. Make sure the spaghetti is not sticking to the bottom of the pot for the first few minutes by stirring
  5. Cook spaghetti until al dente, about 10 minutes
  6. Drain the spaghetti, saving 1 cup of the cooking water for the sauce

Meatballs

  1. Combine the rest of the salt, black and red pepper, minced garlilc, parsley, parmesan and breadcrumbs in a bowl
  2. Add the ground beef to the mixture and combine thoroughly
  3. Fold the 2 eggs into the ground beef mixture
  4. Add the 3 tbsp of olive oil to a pan and bring to medium heat
  5. Form 2 inch spheres from the ground beef mixture to make the meatballs, and place them in the pan to sear
  6. Once the meatballs have been seared on all sides, take them out of the pan and set aside

Sauce

  1. Open the can of San Marzano tomatoes, and add use a spoon to crush the tomatoes inside of the can
  2. Once the tomatoes have been crushed, empty the can into the same sauce pan and oil that you cooked the meatballs in
  3. Add the 1 cup of water saved from cooking the spaghetti
  4. Add the 2 Basil leaves and cook for 2 minutes
  5. Add the meatballs, bring the sauce to a boil, then cover, turn the heat to low and let simmer for 5 minutes
  6. Add the spaghetti to the sauce, or serve side-by-side
